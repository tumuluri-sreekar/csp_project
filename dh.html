Domestic Hygiene:

domestic hygiene (sanitary preparation of food, cleanliness, and ventilation) means cleanliness in your home whereas personal hygiene (proper living habits, cleanliness of body and clothing, healthful diet, a balanced regimen of rest and exercise) means cleanliness of your person or your body.

Domestic hygiene activities include all the work which is done to keep the house and people's clothes and bedding clean. These jobs include sweeping and washing floors, cleaning the toilet, washing clothes and bedding, and washing dishes and cooking utensils after meals. It is important that the house be kept clean so that it is a healthy place. If the house and everything in it are not cleaned often, moisture and dirt gather and it becomes an ideal place for germs, parasites and vectors (disease-carrying animals) to breed and multiply. These germs can cause the people living in the house to get sick.

Activities to maintain Domestic Hygiene....

  • cleaning the toilet

  • washing clothes and beddings

  • sweeping and cleaning floors

  • washing dishes

  • keeping house surroundings clean and green

Cleaning Toilet:

  • Flush, and then add Cleanline Daily Toilet Cleaner or another toilet cleaner around the inside of the bowl. Apply it upwards under the rim, evenly around the outside, so that it trickles down towards the water.

  • Spray and wipe the exterior of the toilet, and the area immediately around it, including the floor and tiles. Dettol Antibacterial Surface Cleanser is ideal for this job, as it kills 99.9% of bacteria.

  • Spray and wipe the seat, under the seat, and the inside of the seat lid. Don’t forget the edge and top of the toilet bowl. Clorox Urine Remover Spray is ideal for this part of the job.

  • Clean bowl using a toilet brush, starting from the top and working downwards in a circle.

  • To finish, press the flush again, holding the toilet brush into the flushing water to clean it. Spray the toilet brush with antibacterial solution, and then store it away.

Washing Clothes and Beddings:

  • First, look through your house for dirty laundry and place in a basket

  • Second, sort through the clothes and separate the lights and dark clothes. Whites are best washed with other whites. Bright colors should be kept together as well.

  • Third, place a pile of sorted clothes into the washer.

  • Fourth, add detergent. Be sure to use the appropriate amount as indicated on the bottle for the type of washing you intend to do.

  • Fifth, set the wash cycle, close the door and press start. After washing, clothes can be hung on a line to dry or placed in a dryer.

Cleaning and Sweeping Floors:

  • Drag the head of the broom along the ground using short, deliberate strokes. Hold the broom in both hands, with one hand at the top and the other near the middle, and move it from the outside of your body inward.

  • Start at the edges of the room and work your way towards the center.

  • Items like hair, lint, and dust bunnies have a tendency to get caught in broom bristles.

  • Sweep the pile you’ve been forming into your dustpan for disposal.

  • Trying to sweep up liquids or soft, runny foods will only spread the mess to other parts of the floor, and could even ruin your broom’s bristles.

Washing Dishes:

  • Consolidate the dishes in the sink or dishpan.

  • Wash the cleanest items or lightest soiled utensils/cookware first.

  • Use a soft sponge or scrubber to wash every dish off all food particles. Ensure you don’t end up scratching the surfaces of your utensils (especially glassware or non-stick pans).

  • Pay attention to the bottom of the pans. Any residue left at the bottom will end up blackening the pan when it would be used next for cooking.

  • Wash knives one by one without hurting yourself. Replace the dishwashing water if it appears greasy or murky.

Keeping House Surroundings Clean and Green:

  • Grooming plants in your garden is an important part of maintaining cleanliness at home. You need to prune plants to prevent them from overgrowing.

  • Overgrown plants may shed leaves in the yard or patio, making it look messy. Keep your lawn well-manicured for a pristine look.

  • Also, make sure you don’t have a vessel or broken pot filled with dirty/rainwater lying in the garden or backyard as standing water is a breeding ground for mosquitoes or other creepy insects.

  • Open windows for a limited time to prevent dust from entering into your home

  • Maintaining cleanliness at home surroundings is not difficult but it does necessarily require time.

Good Domestic hygiene is vital because it helps prevent you and your children from getting or spreading germs and infectious diseases.
